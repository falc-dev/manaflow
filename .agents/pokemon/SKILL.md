## 1. Game Objective & Winning Conditions

### Primary Objective
Knock out six of your opponent's Pokémon to claim all prize cards.

### Starting Conditions
- Each player starts with a **60-card deck**
- Each player draws **7 cards** for opening hand
- Each player puts **1 Pokémon on the Bench** as their Active Pokémon
- Each player takes **6 Prize cards** face-down

### Winning the Game
1. **Knock Out:** Take all 6 Prize cards when opponent's Pokémon is Knocked Out
2. **Opponent Cannot Draw:** If opponent must draw but has no cards
3. **Opponent Concedes:** Opponent chooses to forfeit

### Losing the Game
- Your opponent takes all their remaining Prize cards
- Cannot draw when required
- Choose to concede

## 2. Card Types

### Pokémon Cards

#### Basic Pokémon
- Can be played directly from hand
- Required to evolve other Pokémon
- No previous evolution required

#### Stage 1 Pokémon
- Evolve from Basic Pokémon
- Requires 1 Energy to evolve (usually)

#### Stage 2 Pokémon
- Evolve from Stage 1 Pokémon
- Requires 2 Energy to evolve (usually)
- More powerful effects

#### Pokémon V
- Powerful Pokémon with V rule
- When Knocked Out, awards 2 Prize cards
- Cannot be copied

#### Pokémon VMAX
- Evolved from Pokémon V
- When Knocked Out, awards 3 Prize cards
- Has VMAX rule (cannot be copied)

#### Pokémon VSTAR
- Powerful evolution from Pokémon V
- Has VSTAR Power (special ability)
- When Knocked Out, awards 2 Prize cards

#### Pokémon EX (Legacy)
- Older format card type
- Various rules depending on era

#### Pokémon GX (Legacy)
- Powerful cards with GX attacks
- When Knocked Out, awards 2 Prize cards

#### Pokémon Legend
- Rare, powerful cards
- May count as 2 Prize cards when Knocked Out

#### Pokémon Prime (Legacy)
- Heartgold/SoulSilver era
- When Knocked Out, awards 2 Prize cards

#### Pokémon BREAK
- Evolves from Stage 2
- Keeps previous evolutions

#### Restored Pokémon
- Can be played from discard
- Has different setup rules

#### Baby Pokémon
- Evolves into Basic Pokémon
- Pre-evolution cards

### Energy Cards

#### Basic Energy
- **Fire, Water, Grass, Lightning, Psychic, Fighting, Darkness, Metal, Fairy, Dragon**
- Provides 1 Energy of its type

#### Special Energy
- Provides multiple or special Energy effects
- Usually has drawbacks or restrictions

### Trainer Cards

#### Item
- Use anytime
- Single-use effect
- Most common Trainer type

#### Supporter
- Play only once per turn
- Powerful effects
- Goes to discard pile after use

#### Stadium
- Affects both players
- Stays in play until replaced or discarded
- Only one Stadium in play at a time

#### Tool
- Attaches to a Pokémon
- Provides small bonus or effect
- Stays until KO'd or replaced

#### Pokemon Tool (Legacy)
- Older version of Tool

#### ACE SPEC
- One per deck maximum
- Extremely powerful effects
- Restricted in some formats

**Source:** https://www.pokemon.com/us/pokemon-news/2026-pokemon-tcg-standard-format-rotation-announcement

## 3. Game Zones

### Deck (Library)
- Draw pile
- Face-down
- Must shuffle when required

### Hand
- Cards drawn but not played
- No hand size limit

### Active Pokémon
- Pokémon battling in the active slot
- Takes all attacks from opponent

### Bench
- Reserve Pokémon (up to 5)
- Can be swapped with Active
- Can be attacked (if Active is Knocked Out)

### Prize Cards
- 6 cards taken when opponent's Pokémon is Knocked Out
- Determines winner when all 6 are taken

### Discard Pile
- Used/discarded cards go here
- Can be searched by certain effects

### Lost Zone
- Cards removed from game entirely
- Cannot be recovered

### Stadium Zone
- Current Stadium card in play

### Tool Zone
- Attached Tools to Pokémon

### Energy Zone
- Attached Energy to Pokémon

**Source:** https://www.pokemon.com/us/pokemon-news/2026-pokemon-tcg-standard-format-rotation-announcement

## 4. Energy System

### Energy Types
- **Fire:** Powers Fire Pokémon
- **Water:** Powers Water Pokémon
- **Grass:** Powers Grass Pokémon
- **Lightning:** Powers Lightning Pokémon
- **Psychic:** Powers Psychic Pokémon
- **Fighting:** Powers Fighting Pokémon
- **Darkness:** Powers Darkness Pokémon
- **Metal:** Powers Metal Pokémon
- **Fairy:** Powers Fairy Pokémon
- **Dragon:** Powers Dragon Pokémon

### Energy Attachment
- Attach 1 Energy per turn (unless card effect says otherwise)
- Energy stays attached until discarded

### Energy Costs
- Attacks require specific Energy types
- Must have required Energy attached to use attack

### Retreat Cost
- Number of Energy to switch Active with Bench Pokémon
- Paid by discarding attached Energy

**Source:** https://www.pokemon.com/us/pokemon-news/2026-pokemon-tcg-standard-format-rotation-announcement

## 5. Turn Structure

### 1. Draw Phase
- Draw 1 card from deck
- If cannot draw, lose the game

### 2. Action Phase
- Play Pokémon from hand to Bench (up to 5)
- Evolve Pokémon
- Attach Energy to Pokémon (1 per turn)
- Play Trainer cards
- Use Pokémon's Abilities
- Retreat Active Pokémon (pay Retreat Cost)
- Use attacks (1 per turn per Active Pokémon)

### 3. End Phase
- End of turn effects trigger
- Pass to opponent

**Source:** https://www.pokemon.com/us/pokemon-news/2026-pokemon-tcg-standard-format-rotation-announcement

## 6. Evolution

### Evolution Rules
- Can evolve Basic → Stage 1 → Stage 2
- Evolution requires Energy (not always)
- Cannot evolve same turn a Pokémon is played
- Cannot evolve during first turn in play (unless card says otherwise)
- Evolved Pokémon keeps attached Energy and Tools
- Keeps damage counters

### Evolution Timing
- Can evolve during Action Phase
- Cannot evolve during opponent's turn
- Can evolve before attacking

**Source:** https://www.pokemon.com/us/pokemon-news/2026-pokemon-tcg-standard-format-rotation-announcement

## 7. Attacks & Damage

### Attack Requirements
- Must have Energy cost paid
- Active Pokémon must not be Asleep or Paralyzed
- Only 1 attack per turn per Active

### Damage Calculation
- Attack deals listed damage
- Apply Weakness
- Apply Resistance
- Subtract remaining HP from current HP
- If HP ≤ 0, Pokémon is Knocked Out

### Weakness
- Some Pokémon take extra damage from certain types
- Listed on each card
- Applied before Resistance

### Resistance
- Some Pokémon take less damage from certain types
- Listed on each card
- Applied after Weakness
- Minimum damage is always 1

### Retreat
- Switch Active with Bench
- Pay Retreat Cost in discarded Energy
- Cannot retreat if Pokémon is Asleep or Paralyzed

**Source:** https://www.pokemon.com/us/pokemon-news/2026-pokemon-tcg-standard-format-rotation-announcement

## 8. Special Conditions

### Asleep
- Flip coin at end of turn
- If heads, recover; if tails, stay Asleep
- Cannot attack or retreat

### Confused
- Flip coin to attack
- If heads, attack succeeds
- If tails, attack fails and deal 30 damage to self

### Paralyzed
- Cannot attack or retreat
- Automatically recovers next turn

### Poisoned
- Take 2 damage at end of each turn
- Stacks (takes 4 damage if hit twice)
- Does not wear off

### Branded (Scarlet/Violet era)
- Additional effect from attacks
- Varies by card

### Dodging (Scarlet/Violet era)
- Avoids attacks
- Specific timing rules

### 0 HP Effects
- Some Pokémon have effects when reaching 0 HP
- Triggers before going to discard

**Source:** https://www.pokemon.com/us/pokemon-news/2026-pokemon-tcg-standard-format-rotation-announcement

## 9. Abilities

### Abilities
- Continuous effects
- Can be used once per turn (unless stated otherwise)
- Do not cost Energy
- May have activation costs (discard energy, etc.)

### Poké-Power (Legacy)
- Older version of Ability
- Similar functionality

### Poké-Body (Legacy)
- Continuous effect affecting opponent
- Triggered when specific conditions met

### Ancient Trait
- Special trait on some cards
- Works outside normal rules
- Usually triggers at specific times

### VSTAR Power
- Powerful one-per-game abilities
- Use Counter to mark uses
- Limited to 2 uses per game

**Source:** https://www.pokemon.com/us/pokemon-news/2026-pokemon-tcg-standard-format-rotation-announcement

## 10. Deck Construction Rules

### Deck Size
- Exactly 60 cards (minimum for tournaments)

### Card Limits
- Maximum **4 copies** of the same card (except Basic Energy)
- No limit on Basic Energy
- Only **1 ACE SPEC** card per deck

### Regulation Marks
- Letters indicating format legality
- Found in bottom-left of card
- **G, H, I, J** etc.

### Set Symbols
- Indicate which set card is from

**Source:** https://www.pokemon.com/us/pokemon-news/2026-pokemon-tcg-standard-format-rotation-announcement

## 11. Constructed Formats

### Standard (Most Popular)
- **Deck:** 60 cards
- **Prize Cards:** 6
- **Rotation:** Yearly (April)
- **Legal Sets:** Currently H, I, J regulation marks
- **Cards Removed (2026):** G regulation mark
- Used in most tournaments and championships

### Expanded
- **Deck:** 60 cards
- **Legal Sets:** Black & White (2011) onwards
- **No Rotation:** Cards only banned, not rotated out
- More powerful cards and combos
- Supported in PTCG Live

### Legacy (Discontinued)
- **Legal Sets:** HeartGold/SoulSilver to Legendary Treasures
- **No longer supported** on PTCG Live
- No organized play

### Unlimited
- All cards ever printed
- Extremely powerful (includes early sets)
- No official support

**Source:** https://www.wargamer.com/pokemon-trading-card-game/pokemon-tcg-formats

## 12. Limited Formats

### Sealed Deck
- Build from sealed product
- Tournament: 6 packs
- Build 40+ card deck
- No specific deck size requirement
- Usually 30 minutes per round

### Booster Draft
- Draft from 3 packs
- Pick 1 card, pass rest
- Build 40+ card deck
- Basic Energy usually provided
- 35-45 minutes per round

### Theme Deck (Discontinued)
- Pre-constructed Theme decks only
- Discontinued 2021
- Replaced by Battle Decks

### Battle Deck
- Pre-constructed introductory decks
- Not legal in Theme format
- Good for beginners

**Source:** https://www.wargamer.com/pokemon-trading-card-game/pokemon-tcg-formats

## 13. Unofficial/Community Formats

### Pauper
- Only Common and Uncommon cards
- Budget-friendly

### U150
- 100-card deck
- Only 1 copy of each non-Energy card
- Basic Pokémon max 150 HP

### Aceless
- Standard rotation
- No Pokémon V, VMAX, or GX cards

### Gym Leader Challenge
- Single Pokémon type only
- Singleton (1 copy per card name)
- Black & White onwards

### Region Formats
- Only Pokémon from specific region
- Various house rules

### Unlimited (Pre-2011)
- All cards legal
- Extremely powerful metagame

## 14. Prize Card System

### Prize Cards
- Take 1 Prize card when opponent's Pokémon is Knocked Out
- **Pokémon V:** 2 Prize cards
- **Pokémon VMAX:** 3 Prize cards
- **Pokémon VSTAR:** 2 Prize cards
- Win by taking all 6 (or equivalent)

### First to Prize
- Often race to take prizes
- Can win without Knocking Out all opponent's Pokémon

**Source:** https://www.pokemon.com/us/pokemon-news/2026-pokemon-tcg-standard-format-rotation-announcement

## 15. Tournament Information

### Tournament Structure
- Swiss rounds based on player count
- Best-of-1 in Swiss
- Best-of-3 in Top 8
- Time limits enforced

### Match Play
- Best-of-1: First to 2 Prize cards or win by deck out
- Best-of-3: Win 2 games, not best-of-1

### Rotation Schedule
- **2026 Rotation:** April 10, 2026
- G regulation mark rotates out
- H, I, J regulation marks remain

### Organized Play
- League Challenges
- Championships
- Worlds (annual)
