## 1. Game Objective & Winning Conditions

### Primary Objective
Reduce your opponent's life points to zero.

### Starting Conditions
- Players start with **30 life** (Classic Constructed) or **20 life** (Blitz)
- Each player has a **hero card** that defines their class
- Players start with **weapons and equipment** already in play

### Victory
- Win by reducing opponent's life to 0
- In timed formats, player with most life remaining wins

### Alternative Victory
- Some special cards or abilities provide alternative win conditions
- Living Legend mechanic can affect victory in some formats

## 2. Card Types

### Actions
- Cards played from hand to create effects
- Can be attacks or defensive maneuvers
- Enter the Combat Chain when played

### Attacks
- Specialized action cards focused on offense
- Enter the Combat Chain as attack actions
- Deal damage based on attack power

### Defenses (Defense Actions)
- Cards used to block attacks
- Reduce or prevent incoming damage
- Played in response to attacks in the Combat Chain

### Equipment
- **Types:** Head, Torso, Arms, Legs
- Permanent objects that enhance your hero
- Equipped in designated equipment zones
- Provides passive bonuses and abilities

### Weapons
- Main hand and secondary weapon slots
- Special equipment with attack capabilities
- Can be activated to attack
- May have specific weapon types (Sword, Bow, Dagger, etc.)

### Reactions
- Instant responses to enemy actions
- Can be played during combat
- Modify attack/defense power or add special effects

### Hero Cards
- Defines your class and determines legal cards
- Shows your hero's stats and abilities
- Intelligence stat determines hand size

**Source:** https://tcg-central.com/en/articles/how-to-play-flesh-and-blood-tcg-beginner-rules-guide-2025.php

## 3. Game Zones

### Hero Zone
- Location of your hero card
- Where your character stays throughout the game

### Equipment Zones
- **Head Zone:** Helmets, masks
- **Torso Zone:** Chest armor
- **Arms Zone:** Gauntlets, shields
- **Legs Zone:** Leg armor

### Weapon Zone
- Main weapon slot
- Secondary weapon slot (if applicable)

### Combat Chain
- Active zone for attacks and defenses
- Where combat interactions occur
- Cards remain until combat resolves

### Arsenal
- Storage for one card per turn
- Card stored for future turns
- Can be played for additional effects

### Pitch Zone
- Cards used to generate resources
- Cards pitched go here temporarily
- Return to deck at end of turn

### Graveyard
- Resolved or discarded cards
- Face-up for all players to see

### Ban Zone
- Cards removed from the game
- Typically from powerful effects

### Deck Zone
- Draw pile
- Face-down

**Source:** https://tcg-central.com/en/articles/how-to-play-flesh-and-blood-tcg-beginner-rules-guide-2025.php

## 4. Resource System: Pitch & Action Points

### Pitch (Resources)
- Pitch cards to generate resources
- Usually pitch 1 card = 1 resource point
- Pitched cards go to Pitch Zone
- Pitch Zone resets at end of turn (cards go back to deck)

### Action Points
- Gain 1 action point per turn
- Playing most cards costs 1 action point
- **Go Again:** Some cards grant additional action points
- Can chain multiple plays with Go Again

### Hand Size
- Determined by hero's Intelligence stat
- Usually start with 4-6 cards
- Draw back up to Intelligence at end of turn

**Source:** https://tcg-central.com/en/articles/how-to-play-flesh-and-blood-tcg-beginner-rules-guide-2025.php

## 5. Turn Structure

### Beginning Phase
- No cards can be played
- "At the beginning of the turn" abilities trigger automatically
- Automatic effects resolve

### Action Phase
- **Gain 1 action point**
- Play actions from hand or Arsenal
- Initiate attacks to start combat chain
- Use "Go Again" to recover action points
- Continue until you pass

### End Phase
1. Put Pitch Zone cards back into deck
2. Draw until you have cards equal to hero's Intelligence
3. Pass turn to opponent

**Source:** https://tcg-central.com/en/articles/how-to-play-flesh-and-blood-tcg-beginner-rules-guide-2025.php

## 6. Combat System

### Attack Declaration
1. Play an attack action or activate weapon ability
2. Attack enters the Combat Chain
3. Attack becomes a potential target for defense
4. Calculate total attack power (base + bonuses)

### Defense Step
- Defender can block with cards from hand
- Can use defensive equipment abilities
- Can combine multiple defenses

### Reaction Step
- Both players can play reaction cards
- Modify attack or defense power
- Add special effects to combat
- Trigger synergies with other cards

### Damage Resolution
- Compare attack power to total defense
- Excessive damage = damage to defender's life
- Damage reduces life points

### Combat Chain Resolution
- Attacks and defenses pair off
- Resolved in order
- Damage dealt after resolution

**Source:** https://tcg-central.com/en/articles/how-to-play-flesh-and-blood-tcg-beginner-rules-guide-2025.php

## 7. Deck Construction

### Deck Size
- **Blitz Format:** 40 cards
- **Classic Constructed:** 60 cards

### Deck Requirements
- Must include hero card
- Must include weapon and equipment
- Class-specific card restrictions apply
- No minimum number of action cards required

### Card Restrictions
- Only cards legal for your hero's class
- Some cards restricted by set legality
- Check format-specific legality

### Equipment Rules
- Must have exactly 1 weapon (unless hero allows otherwise)
- Equipment slots: Head, Torso, Arms, Legs
- Can have 1 item per equipment zone

**Source:** https://fabtcg.com/rules-and-policy-center/comprehensive-rules/

## 8. Constructed Formats

### Classic Constructed
- **Deck Size:** 60 cards
- **Starting Life:** 30
- **Rounds:** 55 minutes
- **Best-of:** Best-of-one (Swiss), Best-of-three (Top 8)
- Most competitive format

### Blitz
- **Deck Size:** 40 cards
- **Starting Life:** 20
- **Game Length:** 15-20 minutes
- Faster, more accessible format
- Perfect for beginners

### Living Legend
- Format where hero cards can change based on tournament wins
- Heroes with 20+ Grand Prix wins or 5+ Pro Tour wins enter Living Legend
- Affects which heroes can be played competitively

**Source:** https://fabtcg.com/organised-play/2026/tournament-information-event-structure-and-decklist-requirements/

## 9. Limited Formats

### Sealed Deck
- **Packs:** 8 boosters per player
- **Deck Size:** Minimum 30 cards
- **Hero:** 1 Young Hero from the set
- Build deck from opened cards
- 35 minutes per round
- Best of 1

### Booster Draft
- **Packs:** 3 boosters per player
- **Deck Size:** Minimum 30 cards
- **Hero:** 1 Young Hero from the set
- Draft cards one at a time
- 35 minutes per round
- Best of 1

### Sealed Blitz
- Combination of Sealed and Blitz rules
- 40-card deck from sealed pool

**Source:** https://fabtcg.com/gameplay-formats/sealed-deck/

## 10. Keywords & Mechanics

### Go Again
- Grants additional action point
- Allows chaining multiple plays

### Dominate
- Attacking player gains advantage
- May affect blocking requirements

### Shield
- Prevents damage when blocking
- Different from defense values

### Fusion
- Cards that combine with other cards
- Create powerful combined effects

### Attack Reactions
- Can be played after attacking
- Modify combat after defense

### Defense Reactions
- Can be played when defending
- Enhance blocking

### Instant
- Can be played at any time
- Not restricted by action points (usually)

### Arsenal
- One card stored for later use
- Played from Arsenal when beneficial

### Pitch
- Use card as resource
- Card goes to Pitch Zone

**Source:** https://fabtcg.com/rules-and-policy-center/comprehensive-rules/
